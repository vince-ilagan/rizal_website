# The Republic of Talisay: Rizal's Exile in Dapitan
### PI 10 (Life and Works of José Rizal) — Final Exhibition Project

Moving beyond traditional biographical timelines, this project acts as an interpretive policy matrix—mapping Rizal's mini-republic infrastructure directly against corresponding structural problems and institutional solutions in the modern-day Philippines.

---

## Exhibition Framework: Then & Now

The exhibition isolates four core pillars of Rizal's work in Talisay and contextualizes them within contemporary public goods delivery:

1. **Infrastructure (Water Supply)**: Rizal’s hand-built, gravity-fed Linao spring aqueduct compared alongside localized *bayanihan* rural water setups outside unified municipal utilities.
2. **Medicine (Public Health)**: The Talisay clinic's cross-subsidized care model for the underserved contrasted with the Department of Health's modern *Doctors to the Barrios* (DTTB) program.
3. **Education (Vocational & Experiential)**: Rizal’s non-traditional school for local boys and the collaborative Mindanao Relief Map benchmarked against the K-12 Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) track and the Alternative Learning System (ALS).
4. **Agriculture (Cooperative Economics)**: His 16-hectare estate cultivation and fair-trade abaca draft rules compared to modern agrarian smallholder cooperatives cutting out predatory supply-chain middlemen.
